## What peer-pattern insights are

Peer-pattern insights are **privacy-safe, aggregated cues** about workflow behavior in **similar broad contexts**.

They answer: “In broadly similar conditions, do teams tend to complete prepared actions quickly, or do they often stall without additional follow-through?”

They are **not**:

- “companies like you”
- market research
- a guarantee of outcomes
- an attribution model

## How the peer bucket is chosen

For an account, LeadIntel derives a broad bucket from:

- momentum (rising/steady/cooling)
- first-party intent present vs not present
- data quality (limited/usable/strong)

This produces a stable key like:

`mom_rising_intent_active_quality_usable`

## Privacy model

Cross-workspace insights are only shown when:

- the cohort includes enough distinct workspaces
- the bucket includes enough aggregated events

Otherwise the insight is suppressed with an explicit limitation note.

## What we show

Peer-pattern output includes:

- band (below/within/above norm or insufficient evidence)
- a short summary
- why this account fits the bucket
- confidence label
- privacy note + limitation note
